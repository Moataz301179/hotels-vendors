## Competitor Deep Dives

### 1. MaxAB-Wasoko (Primary Threat)

**Merged:** 2024 (Wasoko acquired MaxAB)  
**Revenue:** $251M (2025)  
**Merchants:** 450,000+  
**Cities:** 50+ across Egypt, Morocco, Kenya, Tanzania  

**Why They Are Dangerous:**
- Massive balance sheet ($190M raised)
- Established supplier network across 6th of October, 10th of Ramadan
- Same-day delivery infrastructure
- Deep VC backing (4DX Ventures, Flourish Ventures)

**Why They Cannot Compete With Us:**
1. **No hospitality SKU taxonomy.** They sell to kiosks and small grocers. Their "shampoo" is consumer Pantene, not 30ml hotel amenities.
2. **No ETA integration.** They operate in informal economy. Hotels need registered tax invoices.
3. **No credit for hotels.** Their BNPL targets shopkeepers, not hotel procurement cycles.
4. **No multi-property governance.** Chain hotels need approval matrices; kiosks don't.
5. **No coastal logistics.** Their network is urban. Red Sea resorts are invisible to them.

**Our Defensive Moat:**
- Vertical depth > horizontal breadth
- ETA-native from day one
- Factoring tailored to hotel cash-flow seasonality
- Authority Matrix for multi-property chains
- Coastal logistics optimization

---

### 2. Capiter (Secondary Threat)

**Status:** Financial distress (2024 layoffs, CEO departure)  
**Revenue:** ~$35M (2024, declining)  
**Merchants:** 60,000  

**Assessment:** Capiter is a cautionary tale. They tried horizontal B2B + payments + credit but spread too thin. Their collapse creates **supplier acquisition opportunity** — we can recruit their former supplier relationships.

---

### 3. TruKKer (Logistics Threat)

**Revenue:** $45M  
**Focus:** Digital freight matching  

**Assessment:** TruKKer solves the logistics piece but has **zero procurement intelligence**. We can partner rather than compete — their trucks, our load-pooling algorithm.

**Partnership Angle:**
- Use TruKKer as fulfillment backbone for non-coastal routes
- Our Eco-Ship algorithm optimizes their fleet utilization
- Revenue share: 70/30 (us/them) on pooled loads

---

### 4. FawryPay (Payments Threat)

**Revenue:** $120M  
**Focus:** Digital payments + merchant acquiring  

**Assessment:** FawryPay is a **potential partner, not competitor**. They want B2B volume; we generate it. Integration opportunity:
- FawryPay handles digital deposit collection (Smart Fix A)
- We handle procurement + factoring + ETA
- Joint go-to-market with EFG Hermes

---

### 5. FutureLog (Global Benchmark)

**Revenue:** €12M  
**Focus:** Hospitality P2P procurement  
**Headquarters:** Germany  

**Why They Are Not a Threat:**
- Zero Egyptian presence
- No ETA integration (EU VAT, not Egyptian tax)
- No factoring partnerships in MENA
- No understanding of Egyptian supplier landscape

**Why We Study Them:**
- Best-in-class UX for hotel procurement
- Strong catalog management
- Good integration with Opera PMS
- **We will exceed their feature set within 12 months**

---

### 6. Amazon Business (Sleeping Giant)

**Revenue:** $35B global  
**Egypt Presence:** Minimal (Amazon.eg is consumer)  

**Assessment:** Amazon Business Egypt is **inevitable but not imminent**. When they arrive:
- They will have industrial SKUs, not hospitality-specific
- No ETA integration (US-centric)
- No local supplier relationships
- No factoring for Egyptian market

**Our Window:** 18-24 months to establish vertical dominance before Amazon Business Egypt launches.

## SWOT Analysis

### Strengths
1. **First-mover in hospitality-vertical B2B in Egypt**
2. **ETA-native compliance** — no competitor has this
3. **Embedded factoring** with EFG Hermes + Contact
4. **Authority Matrix** for multi-property governance
5. **Glassmorphism UX** — differentiated visual identity
6. **6th of October supplier density** — 1,853 factories

### Weaknesses
1. **Pre-revenue** — no track record with suppliers
2. **Small team** — agent swarm model, not full-time employees
3. **No logistics fleet** — dependent on partners
4. **Database on SQLite (dev)** — needs PostgreSQL migration
5. **No mobile app** — web-only for now

### Opportunities
1. **Capiter collapse** — recruit their suppliers
2. **TruKKer partnership** — logistics backbone
3. **FawryPay integration** — payment rails
4. **Saudi expansion** — Sary model validation
5. **Dubai Fintech Hub** — regional factoring licensing
6. **Government tourism initiative** — Hisham Talaat Mustafa Group alignment

### Threats
1. **MaxAB horizontal expansion** — could add hospitality SKUs
2. **Amazon Business Egypt launch**
3. **Economic downturn** — hotel capex freeze
4. **ETA API changes** — compliance disruption
5. **Banking regulation changes** — factoring rules
6. **Currency devaluation** — EGP volatility
